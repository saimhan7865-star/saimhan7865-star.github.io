🇸🇿 Eswatini — Complete Country Guide

🌏 Introduction

Eswatini, officially known as the Kingdom of Eswatini, is a small landlocked country located in Southern Africa. It shares borders with South Africa to the north, west, and south, and Mozambique to the east. Formerly known as Swaziland, the country officially changed its name to Eswatini in 2018.

Eswatini is famous for its rich cultural traditions, colorful festivals, wildlife reserves, scenic mountains, rolling valleys, and welcoming people. Despite its relatively small size, the country offers diverse landscapes ranging from forests and rivers to grasslands and nature parks.

Today, Eswatini's economy is supported by agriculture, manufacturing, mining, forestry, tourism, financial services, renewable energy, and international trade. The government continues investing in infrastructure, education, healthcare, and sustainable development to improve the quality of life for its citizens.

📍 Capital Cities

Eswatini has two capital cities.

  • Mbabane — Administrative and executive capital.
  • Lobamba — Royal and legislative capital.

Mbabane is the country's main business and government center, while Lobamba is home to the royal family, Parliament, and many important national ceremonies.

🏙 Major Cities

  • Mbabane — Administrative capital
  • Lobamba — Royal capital
  • Manzini — Largest commercial city
  • Siteki — Eastern regional center
  • Nhlangano — Southern town
  • Piggs Peak — Mining town
  • Big Bend — Agricultural center
  • Mhlume — Sugar-producing town

👥 Population

Eswatini has a population of approximately 1.2 million people. Most citizens belong to the Swazi ethnic group, while smaller communities of other African and international backgrounds also live in the country.

The nation is known for preserving its cultural identity through traditional ceremonies, music, dance, clothing, and customs.

🗣 Languages

Eswatini has two official languages:

  • SiSwati
  • English

English is widely used in government, education, business, and tourism, while SiSwati is the primary language spoken by most citizens.

💰 Currency

The official currency is the Lilangeni (SZL). The South African Rand (ZAR) is also widely accepted throughout the country.

Both currencies are commonly used in banking, trade, tourism, and everyday transactions.

🌍 Geography

Eswatini covers approximately 17,364 square kilometers. The country features mountains, forests, rivers, grasslands, valleys, and wildlife reserves.

  • Lebombo Mountains
  • Highveld Region
  • Middleveld Region
  • Lowveld Region
  • Malolotja Nature Reserve
  • Mlawula Nature Reserve

🏔 Mountains & Rivers

The western part of Eswatini consists of mountainous highlands with cool temperatures, while the eastern Lowveld features warmer plains. Major rivers include the Great Usutu River, Komati River, Mbuluzi River, and Ngwavuma River.

These rivers provide water for agriculture, hydroelectric power, and local communities.

🌦 Climate

Eswatini has a pleasant subtropical climate. The Highveld enjoys cooler weather, while the Lowveld experiences warmer temperatures throughout the year.

Most rainfall occurs during the summer months, supporting agriculture and wildlife habitats.

🏛 Early History

The Swazi people established powerful kingdoms in the region centuries ago. Traditional leadership, cultural customs, and community life have remained central to Eswatini's national identity.

The country's royal traditions continue to play an important role in modern society.

🏴 Colonial Period

During the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries, Eswatini became a British protectorate known as Swaziland. British administration influenced education, infrastructure, and government institutions.

The country peacefully maintained many of its traditional customs while modern institutions developed.

🕊 Independence

Eswatini gained independence from the United Kingdom on 6 September 1968.

In 2018, King Mswati III officially changed the country's name from Swaziland to Eswatini, meaning "Land of the Swazis."

📈 Economy (Introduction)

Eswatini has a diversified economy supported by agriculture, sugar production, forestry, manufacturing, mining, tourism, financial services, textiles, and international trade.

The country benefits from strong regional trade links with neighboring Southern African nations and continues encouraging investment in renewable energy and industrial development.

🌾 Agriculture

Major agricultural products include sugarcane, maize, citrus fruits, cotton, pineapples, vegetables, timber, livestock, dairy products, and forestry products.

Sugar production remains one of the country's largest export industries.

🏭 Manufacturing

Manufacturing industries include sugar processing, textiles, food processing, beverages, wood products, paper, chemicals, and consumer goods.

⛏ Mining & Natural Resources

Eswatini possesses coal, diamonds, quarry stone, clay, forests, fertile farmland, rivers, and renewable energy resources including solar and hydroelectric potential.

💻 Technology & Innovation

The government continues investing in digital education, telecommunications, renewable energy, financial technology, internet connectivity, healthcare technology, and scientific research to support long-term economic development.

🏙 Urban Development

Mbabane, Manzini, Lobamba, and other towns continue expanding through improved roads, schools, hospitals, residential developments, tourism projects, and commercial investments while promoting sustainable national growth.

🏛 Government

Eswatini is a constitutional monarchy and one of the few remaining monarchies in Africa. The King serves as the Head of State, while the Prime Minister oversees the day-to-day operations of the government.

The country's government operates through the Executive, Legislative, and Judicial branches. Traditional leadership also plays an important role in local governance alongside modern government institutions.

🌍 Foreign Relations

Eswatini maintains diplomatic relations with countries around the world. It is a member of the United Nations (UN), African Union (AU), Southern African Development Community (SADC), Common Market for Eastern and Southern Africa (COMESA), Commonwealth of Nations, and the World Trade Organization (WTO).

The country cooperates internationally in trade, education, healthcare, tourism, environmental conservation, regional security, and sustainable development.

🙏 Religion

Christianity is the largest religion in Eswatini, with many people belonging to Protestant and Roman Catholic churches.

Traditional African beliefs also remain an important part of the country's cultural heritage, and freedom of religion is protected.

🚗 Transportation

Eswatini has a well-developed road network connecting major cities, neighboring countries, and tourist destinations. Railways are mainly used for transporting freight.

Modern highways support trade with South Africa and Mozambique while improving domestic transportation.

Although Eswatini is landlocked, it uses nearby seaports in South Africa and Mozambique for international trade. Road and railway connections provide access to regional ports.

🎓 Education

The government continues investing in schools, universities, vocational education, digital learning, teacher training, and scientific research to improve educational opportunities nationwide.

The University of Eswatini is the country's leading public university and offers programs in science, engineering, agriculture, education, business, and health sciences.

🏥 Healthcare
