🇲🇹 Malta — Complete Country Guide

🌏 Introduction

Malta, officially known as the Republic of Malta, is a small island country located in the central Mediterranean Sea, south of Italy. It consists of three main inhabited islands: Malta, Gozo, and Comino.

Malta is famous for its rich history, ancient temples, beautiful coastline, historic cities, clear blue waters, and strategic location between Europe, Africa, and the Middle East.

Today, Malta has a strong economy driven by tourism, financial services, gaming (iGaming), manufacturing, shipping, and technology.

📍 Capital City

The capital city of Malta is Valletta.

Valletta is one of the smallest capital cities in Europe and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its Baroque architecture, historic fortifications, museums, and cultural landmarks.

🏙 Major Cities

  • Valletta — Capital city
  • Birkirkara — Largest city by population
  • Qormi — Industrial city
  • Mosta — Residential city
  • Sliema — Tourism and commercial hub
  • St. Julian’s — Entertainment center
  • Rabat (Malta) — Historic town near Mdina
  • Gozo (Victoria) — Island capital of Gozo

👥 Population

Malta has a population of approximately 520,000 people. It is one of the most densely populated countries in Europe.

The population is mainly Maltese, with communities of Europeans, Africans, and Asians due to tourism and migration.

🗣 Languages

The official languages of Malta are Maltese and English.

Italian is also widely understood due to historical and cultural ties.

💰 Currency

The official currency is the Euro (€).

🌍 Geography

Malta covers about 316 square kilometers and has a rocky landscape with coastal cliffs, beaches, bays, and small hills.

  • Malta Island
  • Gozo Island
  • Comino Island
  • Grand Harbour
  • Blue Lagoon

🌦 Climate

Malta has a Mediterranean climate with hot, dry summers and mild, wet winters. It enjoys more than 300 days of sunshine per year.

🏛 History

Malta has a long history influenced by Phoenicians, Romans, Arabs, Knights of St. John, French, and British rule.

The country gained independence from the United Kingdom on 21 September 1964 and became a republic in 1974.

📈 Economy

Malta has a highly developed economy based on tourism, financial services, online gaming, maritime services, manufacturing, and technology.

It is known as one of Europe’s fastest-growing digital economies.

🏭 Industries

  • Tourism
  • Online gaming (iGaming)
  • Financial services
  • Shipping & maritime services
  • Pharmaceuticals
  • Electronics manufacturing
  • Information technology

⛏ Natural Resources

Malta has limited natural resources but benefits from its strategic location, marine resources, limestone, and tourism potential.

💻 Technology & Innovation

Malta is a growing digital hub in Europe, investing in blockchain technology, fintech, artificial intelligence, gaming industry regulation, and innovation ecosystems.

🏙 Urban Development

Cities like Valletta, Sliema, St. Julian’s, and Birkirkara continue to expand with modern infrastructure, tourism facilities, and business centers.

🏛 Government

Malta is a parliamentary republic. The President serves as the Head of State, while the Prime Minister is the Head of Government. The country follows a democratic system with a Parliament that makes laws.

Malta is also a member of the European Union (EU), and its laws align with EU regulations and policies.

🌍 Foreign Relations

Malta maintains strong international relations and is a member of the United Nations (UN), European Union (EU), Commonwealth of Nations, Council of Europe, and other global organizations.

The country plays an important role in Mediterranean diplomacy, trade, tourism cooperation, and maritime security.

🙏 Religion

Roman Catholicism is the dominant religion in Malta, and it has a strong influence on culture, traditions, and festivals. However, the constitution guarantees freedom of religion.

🏝 Islands & Geography

  • Malta Island — largest and most populated
  • Gozo — greener and more rural island
  • Comino — small island famous for Blue Lagoon

Malta’s coastline features cliffs, harbors, caves, sandy beaches, and crystal-clear waters, making it a major Mediterranean tourist destination.

🏛 Heritage & History

Malta is home to some of the world’s oldest free-standing structures, including prehistoric temples older than the pyramids of Egypt.

  • Ħaġar Qim Temples
  • Mnajdra Temples
  • Hypogeum of Ħal-Saflieni
  • Mdina — “Silent City”
  • Valletta UNESCO Historic Center

🐠 Marine Life

Malta’s Mediterranean waters are home to diverse marine species, including fish, dolphins, sea turtles, and corals.

  • Groupers
  • Octopus
  • Amberjack
  • Sea bream
  • Dolphins
  • Loggerhead sea turtle

🚗 Transportation

Malta has a road-based transport system, with buses as the main public transport. It has no railways, but ferry services connect islands and nearby countries.

  • Malta Public Transport buses
  • Ferries between Malta, Gozo, and Comino
  • Heliports and sea transport services

✈ Major Airport

  • Malta International Airport (Luqa)

🚢 Ports

  • Grand Harbour (Valletta)
  • Marsaxlokk Harbour
  • Mgarr Harbour (Gozo)

🎓 Education

Malta has a strong education system with public and private schools, vocational institutions, and universities.

  • University of Malta
  • Malta College of Arts, Science & Technology (MCAST)

🏥 Healthcare

Malta offers universal healthcare through public hospitals and clinics, supported by private healthcare services. It is known for high-quality medical care in Europe.

✈ Tourism

Tourism is one of Malta’s most important industries. Visitors come for history, beaches, diving, architecture, and nightlife.

  • Valletta
  • Mdina
  • Blue Lagoon (Comino)
  • St. Julian’s nightlife
  • Gozo Island
  • Ħaġar Qim Temples
  • Golden Bay Beach
  • Blue Grotto

🍽 Traditional Food

  • Pastizzi (cheese or pea pastry)
  • Ftira (Malta bread sandwich)
  • Rabbit stew (Fenkata)
  • Bragioli (beef olives)
  • Kapunata (vegetable dish)
  • Aljotta (fish soup)

🎉 Festivals

  • Carnival of Malta
  • Feast of St. Paul’s Shipwreck
  • Feast of Our Lady of Victory
  • Independence Day (21 September)
  • Republic Day (13 December)
  • Christmas & Easter celebrations

⚽ Sports

Football is the most popular sport in Malta. Water sports, sailing, diving, rugby, and basketball are also widely played.

🔬 Science & Technology

Malta is developing its technology sector, especially in fintech, blockchain, gaming technology, maritime innovation, and digital services.

📊 Economy (Detailed)

Malta has a strong service-based economy driven by tourism, finance, gaming, shipping, and technology. It is considered one of the most stable economies in the Mediterranean.

📦 Imports & Exports

  • Exports: electronics, pharmaceuticals, machinery, services, tourism income
  • Imports: food, fuel, machinery, vehicles, consumer goods

🌱 Environment

Malta focuses on marine conservation, waste management, renewable energy (especially solar power), and protecting its coastal ecosystem.

🏛 UNESCO World Heritage Sites

  • City of Valletta
  • Ħal Saflieni Hypogeum
  • Megалithic Temples of Malta

  • It has no permanent rivers or lakes.
  • Malta has over 300 sunny days per year.
  • It has temples older than the pyramids.
  • English is an official language.
  • Malta joined the EU in 2004.
  • It is a major filming location (e.g., Game of Thrones).
  • It has one of the densest populations in Europe.

  • Malta is a major Mediterranean maritime hub.
