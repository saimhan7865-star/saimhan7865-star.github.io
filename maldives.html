🇲🇻 Maldives — Complete Country Guide

🌏 Introduction

Maldives, officially known as the Republic of Maldives, is an island nation located in the Indian Ocean, southwest of Sri Lanka and India. It is made up of nearly 1,200 coral islands grouped into 26 natural atolls and is famous for its white sandy beaches, crystal-clear waters, luxurious resorts, coral reefs, and rich marine biodiversity.

The Maldives is one of the world's most popular tropical tourist destinations. Its economy is driven mainly by tourism and fisheries, while the country's beautiful islands attract millions of visitors every year.

Today, the Maldives continues investing in sustainable tourism, renewable energy, marine conservation, digital technology, education, healthcare, fisheries, and climate resilience to support long-term national development.

📍 Capital City

The capital city of the Maldives is Malé.

Malé serves as the political, economic, financial, and cultural center of the country. It is home to government offices, the President's Office, major businesses, hospitals, universities, and the country's main international port.

Although small in size, Malé is one of the world's most densely populated cities.

👥 Population

The Maldives has a population of approximately 530,000 people. Most citizens are ethnic Maldivians, with small expatriate communities working in tourism, healthcare, education, and construction.

The population enjoys a strong maritime culture shaped by centuries of trade across the Indian Ocean.

🗣 Languages

The official language of the Maldives is Dhivehi.

English is widely spoken, especially in tourism, business, government, and higher education.

💰 Currency

The official currency is the Maldivian Rufiyaa (MVR).

The Rufiyaa is used throughout the country for banking, trade, tourism, and everyday financial transactions.

🌍 Geography

The Maldives covers approximately 298 square kilometers of land spread across a vast area of the Indian Ocean. It consists of coral islands, lagoons, reefs, beaches, and tropical vegetation.

🏝 Islands & Marine Environment

The Maldives has nearly 1,200 islands, of which around 200 are inhabited. Its coral reefs protect the islands from ocean waves and support one of the world's richest marine ecosystems.

The country's highest natural point is only about 5 meters (16 feet) above sea level, making it the world's lowest-lying nation.

🌦 Climate

The Maldives has a tropical monsoon climate with warm temperatures throughout the year. It experiences a dry season and a rainy season influenced by the Indian Ocean monsoon winds.

Its warm climate supports tourism, fisheries, marine biodiversity, and tropical vegetation year-round.

🏛 History

The Maldives has been inhabited for over 2,000 years and became an important trading center along Indian Ocean trade routes. Islam became the official religion in the 12th century.

The country gained full independence from the United Kingdom on 26 July 1965 and became a republic in 1968.

📈 Economy

The Maldivian economy is driven primarily by tourism, fisheries, transportation, construction, financial services, renewable energy, and international trade.

Tourism contributes the largest share of national income and provides employment for a significant portion of the population.

🌊 Fisheries

Fishing is one of the Maldives' oldest industries. Major catches include tuna, skipjack tuna, yellowfin tuna, reef fish, lobster, and other seafood.

The country promotes sustainable fishing practices to protect marine resources.

🏭 Manufacturing

Major industries include fish processing, boat building, food processing, handicrafts, beverages, furniture production, and construction materials.

⛏ Natural Resources

The Maldives possesses abundant marine resources, coral reefs, fisheries, beautiful beaches, solar energy potential, and rich marine biodiversity.

💻 Technology & Innovation

The Maldives continues investing in digital infrastructure, renewable energy, marine science, climate adaptation, healthcare technology, smart tourism, scientific research, and innovation.

🏙 Urban Development

Malé, Hulhumalé, Addu City, Fuvahmulah, and other islands continue expanding through improvements in transportation, housing, education, healthcare, ports, airports, and sustainable infrastructure.

🏛 Government

The Maldives is a presidential republic. The President serves as both the Head of State and Head of Government. The country operates through the Executive, Legislative, and Judicial branches.

The Constitution guarantees democratic governance, the rule of law, protection of human rights, and supports sustainable national development.

🌍 Foreign Relations

The Maldives maintains diplomatic relations with countries around the world. It is a member of the United Nations (UN), Commonwealth of Nations, South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation (SAARC), Organization of Islamic Cooperation (OIC), World Trade Organization (WTO), and several other international organizations.

The country cooperates internationally in tourism, fisheries, climate change, marine conservation, trade, healthcare, education, and sustainable development.

🙏 Religion

Islam is the official religion of the Maldives, and the majority of the population follows Sunni Islam. Religion plays an important role in the country's culture, traditions, and daily life.
