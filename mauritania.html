🇲🇷 Mauritania — Complete Country Guide

🌏 Introduction

Mauritania, officially known as the Islamic Republic of Mauritania, is a country located in Northwest Africa. It borders the Atlantic Ocean, Western Sahara, Algeria, Mali, and Senegal.

Mauritania is famous for its vast Sahara Desert, ancient trading cities, rich Islamic heritage, iron ore reserves, and unique blend of Arab and African cultures.

Today, Mauritania's economy is mainly driven by mining, fishing, agriculture, livestock, and natural resource development.

📍 Capital City

The capital city of Mauritania is Nouakchott.

Nouakchott is the country's political, economic, and cultural center. It is also the largest city and an important Atlantic Ocean port.

🏙 Major Cities

  • Nouakchott — Capital city
  • Nouadhibou — Major port and commercial center
  • Kiffa — Regional trade center
  • Rosso — Border city with Senegal
  • Atar — Historic desert city
  • Zouerat — Mining city
  • Kaédi — Agricultural center
  • Tidjikja — Historic oasis town

👥 Population

Mauritania has a population of approximately 5 million people.

The population consists mainly of Arab-Berber (Moors), Haratin, and various Sub-Saharan African ethnic groups.

🗣 Languages

The official language of Mauritania is Arabic.

French is widely used in government and education, while Pulaar, Soninke, and Wolof are also recognized national languages.

💰 Currency

The official currency is the Mauritanian Ouguiya (MRU).

🌍 Geography

Mauritania covers about 1,030,700 square kilometers, making it one of Africa's largest countries. Much of the country is covered by the Sahara Desert.

  • Sahara Desert
  • Atlantic Ocean coastline
  • Adrar Plateau
  • Banc d'Arguin National Park
  • Senegal River Valley

🌦 Climate

Mauritania has a hot desert climate with very little rainfall. Southern regions receive more rain than the northern Sahara.

🏛 History

Mauritania has a long history connected with ancient Berber kingdoms, trans-Saharan trade routes, and Islamic civilization.

The country gained independence from France on 28 November 1960.

📈 Economy

Mauritania has a developing economy based on mining, fishing, livestock, agriculture, and petroleum production.

Iron ore exports are one of the country's largest sources of income.

🏭 Industries

  • Mining
  • Iron ore production
  • Fishing
  • Agriculture
  • Livestock
  • Oil and gas
  • Salt production

⛏ Natural Resources

Mauritania has abundant natural resources including iron ore, gold, copper, gypsum, phosphates, fish, and offshore oil and gas.

💻 Technology & Innovation

Mauritania is expanding its telecommunications, digital infrastructure, renewable energy, and e-government services to support economic development.

🏙 Urban Development

Cities such as Nouakchott and Nouadhibou continue to develop through improved roads, ports, housing, and public infrastructure.

🏛 Government

The Islamic Republic of Mauritania is an Islamic presidential republic. The President serves as the Head of State and Head of Government.

The government consists of the Executive, Legislative, and Judicial branches. The National Assembly is responsible for making laws and overseeing national governance.

🌍 Foreign Relations

Mauritania maintains diplomatic relations with countries around the world and is a member of the United Nations (UN), African Union (AU), Arab League, Organization of Islamic Cooperation (OIC), and the G5 Sahel.

The country works closely with neighboring nations on trade, regional security, counter-terrorism, economic development, and environmental protection.

🙏 Religion

Islam is the official religion of Mauritania, and nearly the entire population follows Sunni Islam. Islamic traditions play an important role in daily life, culture, and national identity.

🦓 Wildlife

Mauritania is home to a variety of desert and coastal wildlife.

  • Dorcas gazelle
  • Fennec fox
  • Golden jackal
  • Desert monitor lizard
  • Flamingos
  • Dolphins
  • Migratory birds

🏞 Environment & Climate Challenges

Mauritania faces environmental challenges including desertification, drought, soil erosion, and climate change. Large areas of the country are covered by the Sahara Desert.

The government supports sustainable land management, renewable energy, and conservation of coastal ecosystems.

🚗 Transportation

Road transport is the primary means of travel. Railways, ports, and airports connect major cities and mining regions.

  • National highways
  • Long-distance buses
  • Mauritania Railway
  • Domestic and international flights

✈ Major Airports

  • Nouakchott–Oumtounsy International Airport
  • Nouadhibou International Airport
  • Atar International Airport

🚆 Railways

Mauritania is famous for the Mauritania Railway, one of the world's longest and heaviest freight railways, transporting iron ore from Zouerat to Nouadhibou.

🚢 Ports

  • Port of Nouakchott (Port de l'Amitié)
  • Port of Nouadhibou

🎓 Education

Mauritania has public and private schools, universities, and Islamic educational institutions. The University of Nouakchott Al Aasriya is the country's leading university.

🏥 Healthcare

Healthcare services are provided through government hospitals, regional medical centers, and private clinics. Healthcare facilities continue to improve across the country.

✈ Tourism

Mauritania attracts visitors interested in desert landscapes, ancient cities, Islamic architecture, and nature reserves.

  • Banc d'Arguin National Park
  • Chinguetti
  • Ouadane
  • Atar
  • Richat Structure (Eye of the Sahara)
  • Terjit Oasis

🍽 Traditional Food

  • Thieboudienne
  • Mechoui (Roast lamb)
  • Couscous
  • Camel meat dishes
  • Dates
  • Mint tea

🎉 Festivals

  • Independence Day (28 November)
  • Eid al-Fitr

  • Islamic New Year
  • Prophet Muhammad's Birthday (Mawlid)
  • Traditional cultural festivals

⚽ Sports

Football is the most popular sport in Mauritania. Basketball, athletics, camel racing, and traditional wrestling are also widely enjoyed.

📦 Imports & Exports

  • Exports: Iron ore, gold, copper, fish, petroleum products
  • Imports: Food, machinery, fuel, vehicles, manufactured goods

🌱 Natural Resources

Mauritania possesses abundant natural resources including iron ore, gold, copper, gypsum, phosphates, fish, offshore oil, and natural gas.

🏛 UNESCO World Heritage Sites

  • Ancient Ksour of Ouadane, Chinguetti, Tichitt and Oualata
  • Banc d'Arguin National Park
